Vela

A responsive web application that gives real-time weather, wind and wave predicitons, with up-to-date algae reports. Users can check the reports of their desired location, catch up on the latest news or current trends and share their experiences with others.

Problem Statement

Our weather app users need a way to check current weather related predictions because they need to ensure their safety for the day they chose to participate in a water related activity or sport. We will know this to be true when we see how many users are checking the current weather conditions.

Competitive Analysis

To understand the market for applications that predict the weather for water sports I needed to conduct a competitive analysis of pre-existing applications. I chose Surfline and SailFlow for their similarity in function and their popularity amongst users.

Surfline

Overview :

While Surfline is no doubt a top-dog in marine weather forecasting, it seems to really only cater to the needs of surfers and fisherman. However, being a weall-known source some users feel it has lost its oringinality and become too commercial.

Key Objectives :

"Connecting people with their passion." Surfline/Wavetrak has been a long standing network used by surfers and boaters alike. Having their own products and a team of expert meteorologists and scientists adds an advantage to their brands.

SailFlow

Overview :

SailFlow is designer with sailors in mind, meaning the web application has one purpose. This leaves room for competitors to create a web application for sailing and more. Users have felt the website and app to be too crowded and not user-friendly.

Key Objectives :

"Weather where you sail." SailFlow claims to be the #1 most trusted sailing weather source. Providing real-time updates from a multitude of weather stations, LIVE WIND feature and giving onsite reports from sailors around the world.

Surveys & Interviews

In order to get a general idea of the type of user personas I would need to create for Vela I condudted a series of interviews and surveys. I held in-person and remote interviews with 3 participants and conducted a survey through Google Forms that resulted in 10-15 responses.

Interview Goals

  • Identify user patterns and behaviors around the activity of surfing.
  • Determine which features and functions users would use on a weather app.
  • Discover users' concerns and pain points on a weather app.
  • Collect data on how and why users might use a weather predction app.

Interview Questions

  • At what level would you consider your surfing and approximately how long have you been surfing?
  • How often do you check weather reports before going to surf, if ever?
  • Are you currently or have you ever previously used a weather prediction app, other than weather.com to check the weather before you go surfing?
  • If so, is there anything you would change about the application? If not, would you ever consider using one?
  • Have you ever used an application to record your surf session? If not, would you be open to it, to allow other surfers to get real-time updates?
  • What information do you consider crucial or important to any surfer when planning a session?
  • How can you tell if the weather being reported in reliable?
  • If you were to use a weather prediction application for surfing are there any specific feature you would want to see on it other than predicting the weather?
  • What would be the ideal layout to make reading the weather easy and simple for you as a user?
  • How much level of preparation would you say goes into getting ready to surf?
  • What would you consider at the bottom of your checklist when preparing to surf?
  • Would you like to be a part of a surf community that allows you to share data on surf sessions, competitions or surfing locations?

Insights Gathered

  • Majority of surfers interviewed consider themselves moderate/ intermediate and have been surfing for 10+ years.
  • The intermediate or advanced surfers would always check weather reports before getting in the water.
  • The most important information to them is the size of the swell, direction of wind, air/water temperature and if it is high or low tide.
  • None of the potential users have ever used an applicaton to record surf sessions but the majority were open to the application feature.
  • The addition of a live camera feature was suggested by two out of the three potential users during interviews.
  • No more than 2 hours goes into preparing to surf.
  • Potential users who have used other applications for weather predictions felt it was difficult to find the actual reports, they had to spend a few minutes looking or scrolling to find them.
  • Majority of potential users would join a surf community if it was a feature on the application.

User Personas & Journey Maps

Having gathered information and data from my market research, surveys and interviews I was able to create 2 user personas that reflected the different types of personalities that might use Vela. Using my user personas I created 2 journey maps that represented some of the main functions of the application.

Sitemaps & Sorting

Based on my user personas I created a first iteration sitemap that represented the general organization of the web application. Using card sorting method I was able to create a second iteration sitemap that better represented the applications navigation.

Low-Fidelity Wireframes

First drafts of wireframes that represent the applications 3 main functions; weather prediction, social sharing and news articles/current trends.

Mid-Fidelity Wireframes

Refined wireframes using Balsamiq to illustrate the web applications 3 main functions; weather prediction, social sharing and news articles/current trends.

Usability Tests

After sketching out and designing my screens for the main user flows, I created an interactive prototype with Adobe XD to conduct usability test with 6 participants. Using moderated in-person and remote testing I was able to observe the behavior patterns of my participants while they completed the takss and navigated the prototype.

Rainbow Spreadsheet

Using a rainbow spreadsheet I was able to organize all of my data gathered during my usability tests. I received great feedback from my participants and found points of friction within my application where errors were made. Using these findings I redesigned sections of my web application to improve the quality and functionality of it.

High-Fidelity Wireframes

Finalized wireframes using Adobe XD to illustrate the web applications 3 main functions; weather protection, social sharing and news articles/current trends.

The creation of a design language system and style guide is a key component of the final design stages. To help clearly communicate with developers and stakeholders my design system will act as a guide for the style and aesthetic of my platform.
